My Background

I grew up in rural Colorado after moving from San Diego at age six. With open spaces and endless freedom to tinker, I naturally gravitated toward building and problem-solving from an early age. Whether I was constructing treehouses, disassembling electronics, or piecing together my first computer, I was always driven by curiosity and the satisfaction of figuring things out.

I studied Applied Mathematics at the University of Colorado at Colorado Springs with the original intent of becoming an actuary. But the pull of the tech world—fast-moving, creative, and constantly evolving—proved stronger. What began as a fascination with systems and code quickly turned into a full-blown career, one that has taken me through the evolution from mainframes to data centers and now to the cloud.

After college, I moved to Denver and have called it home ever since. Along the way, I've developed a reputation as a jack of many trades with a hunger to learn. I love a good challenge, especially when it requires learning something entirely new on the fly—whether that’s brewing beer, renovating my house, or rebuilding a car engine. I’ve found that staying ahead in tech means always being a student, and that suits me perfectly. I’m endlessly curious, resourceful, and happiest when I’m deep in a DIY project or solving a tough problem.
